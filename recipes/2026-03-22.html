Glycaemic Index
📊 Low-Medium GI (~45)

Oats have a GI of ~55, but the 6.4g of beta-glucan soluble fibre significantly slows glucose absorption. Adding peanut butter (protein + fat) further blunts the glycaemic response, lowering the combined meal GI to approximately 45. Banana adds ~14g of natural fructose — not a concern at this quantity.

Health Benefits Addressed
📉
Lower LDL Cholesterol: Oats provide ~6.4g of beta-glucan soluble fibre — almost double the 3.4g daily dose shown in clinical trials to lower LDL cholesterol by 5–10%. Beta-glucan forms a viscous gel in the gut, binding bile acids and forcing the liver to draw cholesterol from blood to produce more, directly reducing LDL.
❤️
Cardiovascular Health: Potassium from banana (422mg) supports arterial elasticity. Magnesium from oats and peanut butter (108mg, 34% RDI) is critical for cardiac muscle function and vasodilation. Healthy monounsaturated fats from peanut butter support favourable HDL levels.
🩸
Low Blood Pressure: Potassium:Sodium ratio of 17:1 (422mg K vs 25mg Na) is exceptionally high. Adequate dietary potassium is consistently associated with lower blood pressure by counterbalancing sodium's effects on the kidneys.
Cooking Instructions
1
Add 80g rolled oats and 280mL cold water to a small saucepan. Stir briefly.
2
Heat over medium heat, stirring frequently, for 4–5 minutes until thick and creamy. The oats should have absorbed most of the water.
3
Pour into a bowl. Peel and slice the banana over the top.
4
Dollop 1½ tablespoons of peanut butter in the centre. Dust with ground cinnamon.
5
Serve immediately while hot.
💡 Tip: For a creamier texture, stir 30–40mL of unsweetened soy milk or oat milk through at step 2. It adds negligible cost and boosts the creaminess without any lactose.

Cooking Instructions
1

2

🥦 Broccoli sulforaphane (from glucosinolates): most potent natural anti-cancer compound identified. Don't overcook — max 4 min to preserve glucosinolates. 🐟 Canned salmon with soft bones is the best budget source of calcium (~178mg/can) and omega-3s.

Glycaemic Index
📊 Low GI (~45)

Brown rice has a GI of ~50 (lower than white rice at 72), while frozen peas are very low GI (~48). The high protein from salmon (37g total meal protein) and fat from olive oil and salmon significantly slow glucose absorption. Broccoli adds negligible carbohydrates. Combined meal GI: approximately 45.

Health Benefits Addressed
❤️
Cardiovascular Health: EPA and DHA omega-3 fatty acids (1.5g) from salmon are among the most evidence-backed nutrients for heart health. They reduce triglycerides by 15–30%, decrease inflammation (lowering hs-CRP), reduce platelet aggregation risk, and support healthy heart rhythm. AHA recommends two serves of oily fish per week — this dinner provides the equivalent omega-3 of one full serve.
🛡️
Anti-Cancer: Broccoli's glucosinolates are converted to sulforaphane during chewing — the most extensively studied natural anti-cancer compound, with documented activity against colorectal, breast, and prostate cancers. Garlic's allicin is also anti-carcinogenic. Selenium (50% RDI) from salmon activates glutathione peroxidase, a key antioxidant enzyme that repairs DNA damage.
🩸
Blood Pressure: Omega-3s directly reduce blood pressure by improving endothelial function and reducing arterial stiffness. Garlic allicin inhibits ACE (angiotensin-converting enzyme), the same mechanism as many blood pressure medications. Very low sodium (268mg) with high potassium from broccoli and peas maintains excellent K:Na balance.
💊
Vitamin B12 & D (Gaps Closed): This dinner provides 142% of B12 RDI and 110% of Vitamin D RDI — both nutrients typically deficient in plant-heavy diets. Salmon is one of the richest food sources of both. B12 is essential for nerve function and red blood cell production; Vitamin D supports immune function, calcium absorption, and mood regulation.
Cooking Instructions
1
Start the brown rice first: rinse 75g under cold water, add to a saucepan with 200mL cold water. Bring to a boil, then cover, reduce to lowest heat and simmer 25–28 minutes until water is absorbed. Do not lift the lid during cooking.
2
While rice cooks, cut broccoli into small florets. Don't discard the stem — peel it and slice into coins. Mince the garlic cloves.
3
With 5 minutes left on the rice, add the frozen peas directly to the rice pot (no need to thaw), replace the lid.
4
Heat 2 tsp olive oil in a frying pan over medium heat. Add minced garlic and cook 30 seconds until fragrant (do not brown).
5
Add broccoli florets and 2 tablespoons of water. Toss to coat in garlic oil. Cover with a lid and steam-fry for 3–4 minutes until broccoli is bright green and tender-crisp. Do NOT overcook — this preserves sulforaphane.
6
Open the salmon can, drain the spring water, and add the salmon to the pan. Flake gently. Add smoked paprika and black pepper. Toss together for 1–2 minutes.
7
Squeeze lemon juice over everything. Serve salmon-broccoli mix over the brown rice and peas. Squeeze any remaining lemon on top.
💡 Tip: Pink canned salmon with soft, edible bones is essential — the bones are the calcium source. Mash them in gently during step 6. If your can says "no bones" it will be lower in calcium.
