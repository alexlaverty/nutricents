Glycaemic Index
Very Low GI (~15)

Eggs, avocado, and leafy greens contain negligible digestible starch. Capsicum provides fibre-rich carbohydrates that are digested slowly. This meal produces virtually no blood sugar spike — ideal for maintaining insulin sensitivity.

Health Benefits
❤️
Cardiovascular Health
Avocado provides ~2g oleic acid (MUFA) per serve — the same heart-healthy fat found in olive oil. Combined with egg phospholipids and low saturated fat profile, this breakfast supports healthy lipid metabolism.
🩸
Blood Pressure
820mg potassium from avocado + spinach helps counter sodium's blood pressure effects. Only 230mg sodium. Magnesium from spinach supports vascular relaxation.
🛡️
Anti-Cancer (Allicin + Vitamin C)
Garlic provides allicin — a sulphur compound with anti-cancer and anti-inflammatory properties. Capsicum delivers 110mg vitamin C which neutralises free radicals and supports immune-mediated tumour surveillance.
🧠
Brain & Liver Health (Choline)
3 eggs provide ~390mg choline (87% AI). Choline is essential for neurotransmitter synthesis (acetylcholine), liver function, and is often deficient in Western diets. This is one of the best budget sources available.
Cooking Instructions
1
Prep veg: Dice ½ capsicum into 1cm pieces. Rinse 60g spinach. Slice ½ avocado, drizzle with a few drops of lemon juice to prevent browning.
2
Sauté capsicum: Heat 1 tbsp olive oil in a non-stick pan over medium heat. Add capsicum and cook for 3–4 minutes until slightly softened. Add garlic and cook 30 seconds.
3
Add spinach: Toss spinach into the pan and stir until just wilted (about 1 minute). Push everything to the side of the pan.
4
Scramble eggs: Crack 3 eggs directly into the pan. Using a spatula, gently fold and stir over medium-low heat. Remove from heat while still slightly glossy — residual heat will finish them.
5
Plate & serve: Transfer to a plate. Add sliced avocado on the side. Squeeze remaining lemon juice over the avocado. Season with black pepper and fresh herbs if available.

Glycaemic Index
Low GI (~42 overall)

Chickpeas have a very low GI (~28) and dominate the carbohydrate profile. Pumpkin has a moderate GI (~65) but low glycaemic load due to its high water content. Kale and egg protein further slow gastric emptying. Combined GI is firmly low.

Health Benefits
🛡️
Anti-Cancer (Glucosinolates + Lycopene)
Kale is a cruciferous vegetable that produces sulforaphane and other glucosinolates when chewed — compounds that activate Phase 2 detoxification enzymes that neutralise carcinogens. Mutti tomatoes provide ~12mg lycopene, associated with reduced prostate, lung, and breast cancer risk in epidemiological studies.
👁️
Vision & Immune Health (Vitamin A)
The combined beta-carotene from 400g pumpkin and 170g carrot provides over 3× the daily RDI for Vitamin A — essential for maintaining visual acuity (rhodopsin synthesis), mucosal immunity, and epithelial cell integrity.
📉
Lower LDL Cholesterol (Soluble Fibre)
Chickpeas provide ~6g soluble fibre per serving, which binds bile acids in the gut and forces the liver to use cholesterol to produce more — directly lowering circulating LDL. Pumpkin adds pectin (soluble fibre) for additional effect.
🧬
Anti-Inflammatory (Turmeric + Curcumin)
Turmeric contains curcumin which inhibits NF-κB, a master regulator of inflammation. Combined with black pepper (piperine increases curcumin bioavailability by 2,000%), this soup delivers meaningful anti-inflammatory effect at negligible cost.
Cooking Instructions
1
Prep: Peel and cube pumpkin into 2cm chunks. Dice onion and carrot. Strip kale leaves from stems; roughly chop. Rinse and drain chickpeas thoroughly under cold water.
2
Sauté aromatics: Heat 1 tbsp olive oil in a medium saucepan over medium heat. Add onion and carrot; cook for 4 minutes until softened. Add garlic, turmeric, and cumin; stir for 1 minute until fragrant.
3
Simmer pumpkin: Add pumpkin, chickpeas, and Mutti tomatoes. Pour in 600mL water. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 15 minutes until pumpkin is tender when pierced with a fork.
4
Add kale: Stir in chopped kale. Cook for 3 minutes — it should be bright green and tender but not mushy. Taste and adjust seasoning with black pepper.
5
Egg-drop finish: Reduce heat to low. Crack 2 eggs into a small bowl and lightly beat. Slowly pour the egg in a thin stream around the pot while stirring gently — the eggs will cook into soft ribbons in about 1 minute.
6
Serve: Ladle into bowls. The remaining ½ can each of chickpeas and Mutti tomatoes can be stored in the fridge for 3 days for use in other meals.

Glycaemic Index
Very Low GI (~28)

Lentils are one of the lowest-GI foods on the planet (GI ~25–30). Their dense protein and fibre matrix traps starch granules from rapid digestion. Capsicum, spinach, and carrot add virtually no glycaemic load. Sardine fat and protein further slow gastric emptying. This dinner is ideal for overnight blood sugar stability.

Health Benefits
❤️
Cardiovascular Health (Omega-3 EPA + DHA)
Sardines provide ~2g combined EPA and DHA omega-3 fatty acids — meeting the entire adequate intake in one meal. EPA and DHA reduce triglycerides, lower inflammation markers (CRP, IL-6), and have been shown to reduce cardiovascular event risk by 25–35% in multiple large trials.
☀️
Vitamin D (Rare in Budget Foods)
Sardines are one of the only budget foods that provide meaningful Vitamin D — ~6µg per tin (60% AI). Most Australians are deficient despite sunny climate due to sun-avoidance behaviours. Vitamin D supports calcium absorption, immune function, and mood regulation.
🩸
Iron Absorption Enhancement
Lentils and spinach provide substantial non-haem iron (~5mg total). Critically, the 110mg vitamin C from capsicum dramatically enhances non-haem iron absorption — consuming them together multiplies uptake by 3–6×. This combination is more effective for iron levels than either food alone.
🛡️
Anti-Cancer (Selenium + Allicin)
Sardines deliver ~35µg selenium — a trace mineral that activates glutathione peroxidase, one of the body's most potent antioxidant enzymes. High selenium intake is consistently associated with reduced risk of prostate, lung, and colorectal cancers in population studies.
Cooking Instructions (Assembly — No Heat Required)
1
Prep base: Drain and rinse lentils thoroughly. Dice capsicum and carrot into small cubes (about 0.5cm). Rinse spinach.
2
Warm lentils (optional): For a warm bowl, heat lentils in a saucepan with a splash of water for 3 minutes. For a cold bowl, use straight from the can.
3
Make dressing: In a small bowl, whisk together: 1 tbsp olive oil, juice of ½ lemon, 1 tsp minced garlic, ½ tsp cumin, ½ tsp smoked paprika, and black pepper.
4
Assemble bowl: Place spinach as the base. Add lentils, capsicum, and carrot on top. Drain sardines and flake them over the bowl — include the soft bones (they're edible and are your best source of calcium).
5
Dress and serve: Drizzle the dressing over the entire bowl. Toss gently to combine. The lemon and spice dressing transforms the sardines into a vibrant, flavourful meal.
